# 18. ELITE DECISION MODEL

How top founders decide. Applied to Vitruvian, today.

## The decision hierarchy

Top founders classify every decision along two axes before thinking about the content.

| Axis | Question | Why it matters |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Reversibility | Can I undo this cheaply? | Reversible = fast. Irreversible = slow. |
| Asymmetry | Is the upside uncapped and downside capped? | Asymmetric = take it. Symmetric = skip it. |

```
                 ASYMMETRIC          SYMMETRIC
              ┌────────────────┬────────────────┐
  REVERSIBLE  │  MOVE FAST     │  DELEGATE      │
              │  (1 hour)      │  (not worth    │
              │                │   your time)   │
              ├────────────────┼────────────────┤
IRREVERSIBLE  │  DEEP THINK    │  DEFAULT NO    │
              │  (24–48h max)  │  (skip)        │
              └────────────────┴────────────────┘
```

Most default thinkers treat every decision as irreversible + symmetric. Hence the paralysis.

## When to move fast

**All reversible decisions, resolved inside 60 minutes.** The cost of a wrong call is tiny. The cost of the delay compounds.

| Decision | Reversible? | Target window |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Take an intro call | Yes | Same day |
| Send a cold email | Yes | 15 minutes |
| Try a new headline on the site | Yes | 1 hour |
| Hire a contractor / fractional | Yes (3-month trial) | 1 week |
| Quote a price to a team | Yes (you can renegotiate) | On the call |
| Book a trip to see an athlete or exec | Yes | Same day |
| Publish a LinkedIn post | Yes | 15 minutes |
| Change the deck narrative | Yes | 2 hours |

**You are currently taking days-to-weeks on all of the above. That is the single largest speed leak.**

## When to say no — default

Top founders say no to 95% of inbound and 80% of opportunities they could pursue. Saying no is how capacity gets protected.

| Filter | Say no if… |
|--------|------------|
| Doesn't move the $500M number | Default no |
| Under 10x upside | Default no |
| Requires you personally for ongoing operation | Default no (unless founder-must-do) |
| Consumes irreplaceable time (calendar slot) for small gain | Default no |
| From a generalist with no sports / medtech thesis | Default no |
| A "partnership" that's really an unpaid integration | Default no |
| A custom build for one team | Default no |
| A conference speaking slot without buyers in the room | Default no |
| "Grab a quick coffee to pick your brain" | Default no |

**The underlying rule:** every yes is a no to something else. Treat calendar slots like Series A equity — expensive, finite, accretive only when spent on compounding assets.

## The no-script

You will need these. Rehearse them.

- **To generalist VCs:** "We're raising from strategics with distribution or sports-specific funds. If that changes, I'll reach out."
- **To teams wanting custom builds:** "V1 is one SKU. We'll add custom work at V2 once data volume justifies it."
- **To "pick your brain" requests:** "Happy to trade — what's the intro you can make in return?"
- **To a cousin, a cousin's friend, or anyone recommending features:** "Noted — we're in execute mode this quarter. Adding to the backlog."
- **To early inbound acquirers (low-ball):** "Thanks — too early. Check back at $10M ARR."

## When to move slowly (the only times)

Irreversible + large-consequence decisions deserve 24–48 hours of deliberate thought. Not weeks. Not committees. Structured thinking.

| Decision | Why slow | Process |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Cap-table structure at seed | Permanent | 48 hrs + outside counsel review |
| Co-founder / first VP equity | Permanent | Sleep on it, pre-mortem, check refs |
| Data-rights contract language | Permanent | IP attorney review, specific clauses |
| Accepting a strategic investor (vs declining) | Hard to reverse — they're on the cap table | 48 hrs + reference calls |
| Naming / rebranding | Sticky | 48 hrs, tested with 5 buyers |
| Patent claim strategy | Permanent at issuance | Full week, with patent counsel |

Note: "Should I build feature X?" is not on this list. Features are reversible. Decide in a day.

## How top founders minimize risk — the techniques

### 1. Pre-mortem

Before any big decision: "It's 12 months from now. This went badly. What killed it?" Write 5 causes. Address the top 2.

Applied to Vitruvian's seed raise:
- *What kills it?* (1) Wrong cap-table partners, (2) valuation set too high, forcing a down round, (3) no strategic on the cap table, (4) data rights given up in side letters, (5) 24-month runway instead of 30.
- *Address:* (1) Only strategics + sports-specific funds. (2) Raise at $15–20M pre, not $30M. (3) One strategic check mandatory. (4) Counsel reviews every side letter. (5) Raise 30-month runway minimum.

### 2. Downside capping

Never make a bet where the downside exceeds 10% of your total runway or equity. If it does, restructure until it doesn't.

| Bet | Downside uncapped? | Fix |
|-----|---------------------|-----|
| $100K inventory build | Yes (if no customers) | Get 3 deposits first |
| First hire $180K/yr | Yes | Fractional at $8K/mo, 6-month trial |
| Conference booth $40K | Yes | Share booth with complementary vendor |
| Athlete equity 0.5% | No (dilution capped) | Take without hesitation |

### 3. Position sizing

Not all bets are the same size. Elite founders spend the most on what has the highest expected value × probability × reversibility.

| Bet | Size | Why |
|-----|------|-----|
| Data-rights clause in every contract | Maximum (every deal, no exceptions) | Cheap, high EV, permanent |
| Athlete equity | Multiple small positions (3–5 athletes) | Each is long-tail |
| Strategic investor | One anchor | Concentration needed |
| Feature build | Small | Reversible, defer until post-seed |
| Media / content | Small daily, compound | Zero downside |

### 4. Reversible experiments before irreversible commits

Test every big move with a cheap reversible version first.

| Big move | Cheap test |
|-----------|-------------|
| Full partnership with Hyperice | 1 joint pilot with one team, 90 days |
| Hire a VP Sales | 3-month fractional engagement |
| Open an EU market | 1 trip, 5 meetings, reversible |
| Launch consumer SKU | DON'T (doc 11 forbids this) |

### 5. Bias against analysis, bias toward evidence

Top founders replace "let me think about it" with "let me test it for a day." Data beats debate.

| "I'm thinking about…" | Replace with… |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| Whether the pitch resonates | Send it to 10 people today |
| Whether $3K/mo is right | Quote it to the next 3 calls |
| Whether the name lands | A/B test the landing page this week |
| Whether the market is ready | Book 5 discovery calls this week |

## The one sentence

**Move fast on reversible + asymmetric. Say no by default to symmetric. Slow down only on irreversible, and never for more than 48 hours.**

Every founder who compounded past $500M internalized this grid. Every founder who stalled at $5M treated every decision as irreversible + symmetric — and paid the time cost forever.
