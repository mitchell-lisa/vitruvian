# 13. THE REAL MONEY GAME

Where money actually moves in sports tech — and where you're currently standing in that chain.

## The top of the pyramid

Nobody in pro/college sports is writing checks with their own money. The cash comes from one place and waterfalls down.

```
MEDIA RIGHTS ($200B+ US pro sports, 2024–2034)
        │
        ▼
  LEAGUES (NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, NCAA)
        │
        ▼
  TEAM OWNERS (franchise equity appreciating 10–30x / 20 yrs)
        │
        ▼
  TEAM BUDGETS ($5–20M/yr per pro team, performance slice = $500K–$2M)
        │
        ▼
  HEAD OF PERFORMANCE + DIRECTOR OF SPORTS MEDICINE (~2 humans/team)
        │
        ▼
  VENDORS (Hyperice, Therabody, Catapult, WHOOP, Normatec, you)
```

You are at the bottom, fighting for 0.1% of a team's budget. That's the whole game if you let it be.

## Who controls distribution

| Channel | Controlled by | How they got it | What it costs to break in |
|---------|---------------|------------------|-----------------------------|
| Pro team training rooms | Hyperice, Therabody, Catapult, WHOOP | Athlete endorsements + 10 yrs of relationships | $10M+ in BD / athlete deals |
| College athletic depts | Same incumbents + Nike/UA contracts | School apparel deal bundles tech | Nike partnership or bust |
| PT clinics (~40k US) | McKesson, Henry Schein, Patterson (medical distributors) | Decades of distributor networks | Distributor partnership |
| Consumer / DTC | Amazon (~50%), Best Buy, Dick's, Apple Health | Shelf space + algorithm | $50M+ marketing spend |
| Athlete endorsements | CAA, Klutch, Excel, WME (10–20% cut) | Agent relationships | Agent relationship |
| Insurance / workers' comp | Liberty Mutual, Chubb, Zurich | Actuarial moats + regulatory licenses | Prove injury reduction, share savings |
| Sportsbook data feeds | DraftKings, FanDuel, Genius Sports | Exclusive league deals | License to Genius or Sportradar |

**None of these are you.** Every one of them is a middleman you either pay or partner with.

## Where profits actually concentrate

Ranked by who captures the most value per dollar of end-user spend:

| Rank | Player | Gross margin | Moat | Why they win |
|------|--------|--------------|------|--------------|
| 1 | Team / franchise owners | — | Scarcity (32 NFL teams) | Equity appreciation, not operating profit |
| 2 | Media rights holders | 40–60% | Exclusive league contracts | Source of all sports money |
| 3 | Top agents (CAA, Klutch) | 10–20% take | Athlete relationships | Control who endorses what |
| 4 | Brands with athlete lock-in (Nike, UA) | 45%+ | Endorsement contracts + shelf | Distribution + aspiration |
| 5 | Sports PE (Arctos, RedBird, Dyal) | — | Capital access | Buy ancillary sports biz at 15x+ |
| 6 | Insurance writers | 90%+ | Actuarial + regulatory | Underprice risk, pocket spread |
| 7 | Sportsbooks / data aggregators | 80%+ | Exclusive feeds, regulatory | Sell edge to bettors |
| 8 | Platform data companies (WHOOP, Catapult) | 70%+ | Switching cost + data lock-in | Every athlete wears one |
| 9 | Endorsement-driven DTC (Therabody, Hyperice, Gymshark) | 50%+ | Athlete marketing + shelf | Consumer buys because pros use it |
| 10 | Equipment vendors to teams (← you, today) | 20–35% | Relationships, slow to build | Thin margin on hardware |

**Sitting at #10 is a 20-year grind to $50M.** Sitting at #6, #7, or #8 is a 5-year sprint to $500M+.

## The hidden value chain nobody talks about

Beneath the visible "sell machines to teams" layer, there are four invisible money rivers flowing 10–100x larger:

| River | Annual US size | How you enter |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Injury cost avoidance** (team insurance, workers' comp, disability) | $3B+ premium written on sports alone | Prove injury reduction with data → share savings with insurer |
| **Athlete contract preservation** (longevity = guaranteed $) | $20B+ in active guaranteed contracts, NFL alone | Team Head of Performance protects $ — frame pitch as contract insurance |
| **Betting market edge** (biometric data → in-game lines) | $150B+ US handle 2024 | License anonymized movement / fatigue data to Genius Sports / Sportradar |
| **Franchise valuation premium** (tech-forward teams sell for more) | Hundreds of millions per sale | Position as part of the "modern franchise" acquirer narrative |

Right now your pitch enters none of these rivers. It enters the shallowest one — vendor to a team budget.

## Who captures value in YOUR current design

| Layer | Who captures | How much | Why |
|-------|--------------|----------|-----|
| Athlete uses machine | Team (better performance) | Large, unquantified | They own the roster |
| Team trains the athlete | Agent / league / owner (equity) | Massive | Performance = contracts = media value |
| Data generated during session | **You, if contracted right. Team, by default.** | $0 today | You haven't claimed it |
| Injury prevented | Team insurer (lower premium) | $100K–$1M per avoided injury | They pay the claim |
| Brand narrative ("elite athletes use it") | Hyperice / Therabody | $10M+ per top-5 athlete endorsement | They own shelf + marketing |
| Hardware margin | You | $20K–$35K per machine | Linear, solo, slow |

**Default ownership without intentional structuring:** team gets the benefit, insurer gets the savings, Hyperice-style brand gets the marketing — you get 30% hardware margin and support calls.

## How to move up the value chain

| Current position | Move | Captures |
|-------------------|------|----------|
| Vendor to teams (#10) | Sign data-rights-in-perpetuity into every team contract | Platform data company (#8) |
| Platform data company (#8) | Productize injury-risk model, sell to insurers | Insurance arbitrage (#6) |
| Insurance arbitrage (#6) | License anonymized movement data to Sportradar / Genius | Data aggregator (#7) |
| All of the above | Take athlete equity deals in exchange for team access | Endorsement distribution (#4) |
| All of the above | Structure as acquisition target for Hyperice/Therabody/Nike | Exit at brand-multiplier valuation |

Each move is a contract clause, not a technology build.

## The three clauses that change your position in 24 months

1. **"Vendor owns all data generated by the device in perpetuity, in anonymized form, for training, licensing, and resale purposes."**
2. **"Vendor is granted right of first refusal on any athlete endorsement deal arising from use of the device."**
3. **"Vendor may license aggregate, anonymized injury-risk outputs to third-party payors and data aggregators."**

Those three clauses, signed by your first 5 team customers, are worth more than the next 100 machines you sell.

## The one paragraph that summarizes the whole game

Sports money comes from media, waterfalls through leagues, concentrates in franchise equity, and trickles down to team budgets where you fight for scraps alongside every other vendor. The actual high-margin rivers — insurance arbitrage, betting data, endorsement distribution, and acquisition premium — flow invisibly beneath the visible "sell hardware" layer. You are currently standing in the shallowest river. Changing which river you stand in is a legal and positioning decision, not a product one. Write it into the contract before the first pilot signs, or you give away the business for a 30% hardware margin forever.

## The one question to ask every time money is about to move

**"Who is capturing the delta I'm creating — and can I contract my way into their seat?"**
