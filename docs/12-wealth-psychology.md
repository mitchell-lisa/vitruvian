## The four loops that cap your income

1. **Craftsman > Founder.** You love building. You hate selling. So you build, call it progress, and the bank account never moves. **Craftsmen make $200K. Founders make $200M.**

2. **Permission > Conviction.** You ask others what they think before acting. Investors fund conviction, not consensus. Every "what do you think?" you outsource is a dollar of valuation you give away.

3. **Control > Leverage.** Hardware solo = fully in control, tiny upside. Software + team + partners = less control, 100x upside. You keep choosing control. The pattern is legible across every decision in this repo.

4. **Worthiness > Outcome.** The hard-mode path (hardware, solo, patent-first, pilot-before-revenue) feels like it earns the win. Markets don't reward worthiness. They reward leverage. **Stop trying to deserve it.**

## What's limiting your income — in one paragraph

You are optimizing to be seen as a careful, thoughtful, credible builder — and you are achieving it. That identity caps you at ~$300K/yr forever. The $500M founder identity requires being seen, at least occasionally, as aggressive, commercial, uncomfortable to sit across from, and willing to be wrong in public. Until you're willing to trade "careful" for "consequential," the hardware will be beautiful and the bank account will be modest.
