# 14. POWER THINKING FRAMEWORK

How wealthy founders think — and where your default thinking loses money.

## Three axes

| Axis | Default thinker | Power thinker |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| Decision speed | Days–weeks to decide | Hours for reversible, 48h max for irreversible |
| Risk tolerance | Avoids public failure (=ego cost) | Avoids private mediocrity (=$ cost) |
| Opportunity recognition | "Is this a good idea?" | "Is the downside capped and the upside uncapped?" |

## Decision speed — the math

Founders who close $10M+ make ~10x more decisions per week than "careful" founders. Same information, more shots.

| Decision type | Power thinker window | Your current window |
|----------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| Hire a contractor | 1 hour | 2 weeks |
| Price the product | 24 hours | Still open, 6 months in |
| Take an intro call | Same day | "Let me check my calendar" |
| Sign a pilot | 48 hours after verbal | Lawyer redline, 3 weeks |
| Cut a feature | Mid-standup | Discussed across 4 docs |
| Fire a supplier | That week | Endure |

**Rule:** If the decision is reversible and costs under 10% of monthly burn, you have 60 minutes, not 6 days.

## Risk tolerance — the inversion

Default thinkers ask: "What could go wrong?"
Power thinkers ask: "What's the cost of not doing this, compounded over 10 years?"

| Situation | Default fear | Power calculation |
|-----------|---------------|--------------------|
| Cold-email 50 VCs | "I'll look desperate" | "50 NOs = 0 cost. 1 YES = $3M." |
| Quote $3K/mo to a team | "They'll laugh" | "Worst case: they counter. Best case: $36K/yr ARR." |
| Name a $500M outcome publicly | "I'll look stupid if I miss" | "Missing $500M by shooting for $500M > hitting $5M by shooting for $5M." |
| Sign data-rights clause | "Teams might push back" | "Not having it = $0 data asset forever." |
| Take athlete equity | "Feels transactional" | "0.5% for $5M of distribution = 1000x leverage." |

## Opportunity recognition — skew, not comfort

Power thinkers filter every opportunity through one lens: **skew**. They pass on symmetric bets and pile into asymmetric ones.

| Opportunity | Risked | Payoff | Skew | Power move |
|-------------|--------|--------|------|------------|
| $10K bet, $10M upside | $10K | $10M | 1000x | Take it without thinking |
| $100K bet, $150K upside | $100K | $150K | 1.5x | Decline — not worth thinking time |
| Athlete equity 0.5% of $500M company | Dilution on a future win | Top-of-funnel forever | Uncapped | Offer it today |
| Spending 20 hrs on patent-figure polish | 20 hrs | Zero revenue change | Negative | Kill immediately |
| 3-hour dinner with a Hyperice VP | 3 hrs | Distribution to 30 NFL teams | 100x | Book this week |

## The five mental moves wealthy founders make that you don't

1. **Regret minimization over risk minimization.** Bezos's test: "In 40 years, will I regret not trying this?"
2. **Inversion.** Munger's test: "What would cause me to fail — and what if I just didn't do those things?" (Doc 11 lists your anti-patterns. Read them weekly.)
3. **Expected value > comfort.** 90% chance of $0 and 10% chance of $10M has EV $1M. Default thinkers see 90% loss. Power thinkers see $1M.
4. **Time as the rarest asset.** Every hour on something that doesn't compound (copy polish) is an hour stolen from something that does (capital, contracts, distribution).
5. **Status vs outcome.** Default = optimize for being seen as smart. Power = optimize for outcome even when it looks dumb for 18 months.
