# 19. MILITARY / DoD GO-TO-MARKET

Non-dilutive capital + the best-paying MSK buyer in the country. Parallel pathway, not a pivot.

## Why DoD is the right buyer

| Metric | US pro sport | US DoD |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| MSK injuries / yr | ~8K (pro leagues combined) | ~800K (active-duty, all services) |
| MSK cost / yr | ~$1.2B (guaranteed contracts lost) | ~$3.7B (readiness + medical) |
| Buying authority | 1 GM / program | SBIR, OTA, MIPR, base-level |
| Contract speed | 6–18 months | 90 days (SBIR Phase I) |
| Dilutive? | Yes (you sell hardware) | No (SBIR is grant + contract) |

MSK injury is the #1 cause of lost duty days in every service branch. It is a larger, faster-paying, data-richer market than pro sport — and it runs on non-dilutive capital.

## AFWERX — the fastest yes

The AFWERX Open Topic SBIR is the easiest point of entry. Quarterly solicitations. 10-page application. Three-judge panel. Funded in 90 days.

| Step | Action | Owner | Window |
|------|--------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Register SAM.gov + DSIP | Founder | 1 week |
| 2 | Identify customer sponsor (AF squadron or wing) | BD | 2–4 weeks |
| 3 | Draft Phase I proposal (10 pages) | Founder + SBIR consultant | 2 weeks |
| 4 | Submit to Open Topic | Founder | Next window |
| 5 | Oral pitch (if shortlisted) | Founder | 30 days after submit |
| 6 | Phase I award ($75K, 3 months) | Contract office | 30–60 days |
| 7 | Deliver Phase I report + deploy at base | Team | 3 months |
| 8 | Phase II application ($1.7M) | Founder | During Phase I |

**Target squadrons for customer sponsorship:** pararescue (PJ) units, combat controllers, air combat command fighter squadrons, pilot training bases (MSK prevalence is >50% in these populations).

## SOFWERX — the best-paying pilot

USSOCOM operators are the closest analog to elite pro athletes. Highest MSK load, highest cost per loss, most forward-leaning on performance tech.

| What SOFWERX buys | What we offer |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Force readiness — reduce op-tempo injuries | Session-level MSK risk signal per operator |
| RTD acceleration post-surgery | Objective ROM + asymmetry restoration timeline |
| Data for Tactical Human Optimization (THOR3) | Longitudinal mobility corpus across SOF units |

Entry: Tech Experimentation (TE) event, apply 60 days out, demo on-site at SOFWERX facility in Tampa. If selected, CRADA + funded pilot within 180 days.

## DIU — the biggest check, hardest to win

DIU buys at scale but expects commercial-first. They fund prototype OTAs only when a company already has private revenue. Track this pathway for **after** the seed round closes and there is paying pilot revenue.

| Prerequisite | Our status |
|---------------|-------------|
| Commercial customers | Pilot LOIs in hand — 6 mo out |
| Patent filed | Yes — provisional filed |
| Dual-use story | Yes — sport + military natural fit |
| Ready to deploy in 12–24 mo | On seed closing — yes |

## DHA — the clinical validation play

DHA funds clinical research on MSK injury reduction. Longer, but the output is peer-reviewed evidence that unlocks every downstream insurer and league deal. Target: Uniformed Services University (USUHS) + Consortium for Health and Military Performance (CHAMP).

| Output | Downstream value |
|--------|------------------|
| Peer-reviewed paper — ROM/asymmetry predicts MSK injury | Validates insurance licensing claim |
| Multi-site protocol | Repeatable in pro / collegiate |
| DoD citation | Credibility with strategics |

## Military-specific product deltas

The hardware ships identical. The protocol library and reporting ship different.

| V1 commercial | V1 military |
|---------------|-------------|
| Pro sport stretch protocols | Tactical athlete baseline + unit-level aggregates |
| Team-level dashboard | Squadron / platoon readiness dashboard |
| AMS integration (Smartabase, Kitman) | DoD data standards — HL7, MHS GENESIS pathway |
| Per-athlete report | Per-operator + anonymized unit rollup |
| Cloud optional | Gov cloud (IL-4) or on-prem only |

Minimal engineering lift. ~2 person-weeks of integration work per pathway.
