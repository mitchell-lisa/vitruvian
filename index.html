The Problem

Flexibility is the one variable performance medicine still estimates by eye.

Force plates measure power. GPS measures output. HRV measures recovery. Nothing measures the tissue that actually tears — not at matched load, not longitudinally, not at scale.

Soft-tissue injury remains the leading cause of lost time across elite sport and high-performance military units alike. The data to predict and prevent it has never been collected.

The Machine

Strap in. Get stretched. Get measured.

Step 01

Strap in

The athlete anchors into a guided position — no clinician, no partner required. Setup takes under a minute.

Step 02

The machine stretches

A force-controlled cable takes the athlete through calibrated range. Identical, repeatable load every rep. Backdriveable and safe by design.

Step 03

It feels the tension

Onboard sensors read resistance, end-range tolerance, and the exact point tissue begins to yield — continuously, through the whole movement.

Step 04

It detects deficiencies

Vision tracking catches the pelvic rotation, trunk shift, and asymmetry that mask true range — surfacing the compensations that drive re-injury.
