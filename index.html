Platform Overview

Standardized stretch delivery. Quantified outcomes.

Vitruvian is a cable-assisted mobility platform that delivers repeatable stretch protocols at controlled loads, measures joint ROM to within 2 degrees, and flags bilateral asymmetries automatically.

A depth camera tracks compensation in real time. Load cells measure tissue tolerance at 100Hz. Every session generates objective data that syncs to your athlete management system.

Vitruvian does not replace your therapists. It extends what they can do — running the same protocol on 20 athletes in a morning with zero variability, zero fatigue, and complete documentation.

Your staff still supervises. They just stop being the bottleneck.

What It Measures

Every session generates objective data.

Joint Angle (ROM)

Depth camera + cable geometry measure peak passive range of motion at standardized force thresholds. Comparable across sessions and athletes.

Bilateral Asymmetry

Left vs. right comparison on every stretch. Flags imbalances exceeding clinical thresholds. Tracks resolution over time.

Force at End-Range

Inline load cell measures cable tension at peak ROM. Indicates tissue stiffness and stretch tolerance — not just flexibility.

Hold Tolerance

Duration the athlete maintains end-range without retreating. A neuromuscular metric — measures relaxation response, not just joint range.

Compensation Detection

Depth camera identifies pelvic rotation, trunk lateral flexion, and knee bend during SLR. Flags compromised stretch quality in real time.

Session-over-Session Trend

Rolling 4-week and 8-week ROM averages. Separates acute stretch response from chronic flexibility change. Evidence, not anecdote.

V1 Protocol Library

Lower extremity — where 80% of athletic mobility work occurs.

Hamstring SLR

Position: Supine on bench
Cable: Ankle cuff lifts leg
Stabilization: Pelvis braced, opposite leg anchored

Hip Flexor / Quad

Position: Side-lying or prone
Cable: Thigh cuff extends hip
Stabilization: Pelvis braced anteriorly

Adductor

Position: Supine
Cable: Ankle cuff abducts leg
Stabilization: Pelvis and contralateral leg anchored

Piriformis / Ext. Rotation

Position: Supine, knee flexed 90°
Cable: Ankle cuff applies rotation
Stabilization: Knee and pelvis controlled

Calf / Soleus

Position: Standing or supine
Cable: Foot plate dorsiflexion
Stabilization: Knee position controlled (bent vs. straight)

PNF Contract-Relax

Method: 5s isometric against cable, then 30s passive advance
Detection: Force spike confirms contraction quality
Cycles: 3 per muscle group
