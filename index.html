What Is Vitruvian

A stretching machine that adapts to your body — in real time.

Vitruvian is a standing stretching station where an athlete steps in, attaches padded cuffs connected to motorized cables, and follows a guided routine on a touchscreen.

Force sensors and a depth camera track your range of motion in real time. The software adjusts cable tension and stretch targets based on how your body is actually responding — not a generic preset.

Think of it as a StretchLab therapist replaced by a machine that never forgets your limits, never pushes too hard, and is available 24/7.

It belongs in an NFL training room, a physical therapy clinic, or a high-end gym — not a sci-fi movie.

How It Works

Step in. Get stretched. Get better.

01

Step In

Walk up to the open frame. Tap the touchscreen or scan your phone. The machine recognizes you and loads your profile.

02

Clip In

Attach padded ankle or wrist cuffs to the cable carabiners. The machine confirms each connection with a gentle test pull.

03

Get Stretched

Follow the guided routine. Motorized cables apply progressive tension — slowly pulling you deeper into each stretch while monitoring your limits.

04

Adapt + Progress

The system tracks your range of motion over time. Each session builds on the last — nudging you 1-2 degrees further, safely and measurably.

Specifications

Built with technology that exists today.

Frame

7 ft tall x 5 ft wide x 4 ft deep. Steel or aluminum extrusion. Open-front design — no enclosure.

Drive System

4 brushless DC motors (200W, 24V) with 20:1 planetary gearboxes. Smooth, quiet, precise torque control.

Force Control

Inline load cells on each cable. 100Hz sampling. PID compliance loop. 30 lb software cap + 35 lb mechanical slip clutch.

Vision

Depth camera with real-time pose estimation. Tracks 12 key joints. Calculates joint angles for form correction.

Interface

15" capacitive touchscreen. Guided routines with real-time ROM gauge, tension indicator, and form prompts.

Safety

8-layer defense system: software ramp limits, PID compliance, force caps, slip clutches, E-stop, quick-release cuffs.
