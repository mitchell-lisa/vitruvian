01

The Problem

Stretching is the last unquantified discipline in elite sports.

Strength has force plates. Speed has timing gates. Recovery has Normatec. But flexibility — the thing that prevents injuries and extends careers — is still done by hand, estimated by eye, and documented on paper.

No Measurement

Manual goniometry has 5–10° measurement error. Two testers, two different numbers. No way to track real change over time.

No Consistency

Stretch quality depends on which staff member is available, how tired they are, and how many athletes they've already seen that day.

No Data

When an athlete tears a hamstring, there's no longitudinal flexibility data to analyze. The warning signs were never captured.

How It Works

Eight minutes. Nine variables. Zero guesswork.

1. Badge in

Athlete scans ID. System loads their profile, history, and today's training context.

2. Baseline screen

Standardized passive stretch at 8 lb each side. Bilateral ROM comparison in 60 seconds.

3. Stretch protocol

Motor ramps cable tension. PID loop maintains constant force. Camera tracks compensation. Data logs every second.

4. Session report

ROM gain, asymmetry change, tolerance trend. Exportable. Comparable. Actionable.

04

Technology

Five systems. One closed loop.

Cable Drive

200W BLDC motor, 20:1 planetary gearbox, Dyneema cable. ODrive FOC controller at 100Hz. Force-compliant, backdriveable.

Depth Camera

Intel RealSense D435. 12-point skeletal tracking at 30fps. Measures joint angles to ±2°. Detects compensation in real time.

Live Interface

15" touchscreen. Real-time force graph, ROM tracking, bilateral comparison. Dark UI designed for staff and athlete visibility.

Quick-Release Cuff

Padded neoprene. Carabiner quick-release. 5-layer safety: software cap, PID compliance, slip clutch, E-stop, quick-release.

05

What It Measures

Nine variables. Measured, not estimated.

Joint Angle

Peak passive ROM at standardized force. ±2° accuracy. Comparable across sessions.

Cable Force

Inline load cell at 100Hz. Logs tension ramp, peak force, and variability during hold.

Asymmetry

Left vs. right at matched force. Flags differences >10%. Tracks resolution over time.

Hold Time

Duration at end-range without retreating. A neuromuscular tolerance metric.

Compensation

Camera detects pelvic rotation, knee bend, trunk shift. Quantified, not eyeballed.

Tolerance Threshold

The force level where the athlete first reports discomfort or resists. Tracked over weeks.

Pain Response

Binary flag per rep. Correlated with force and ROM to build a tissue-response profile.

Tension Ramp

Rate of force acceptance during initial stretch. Indicates tissue readiness and stiffness.

Session Δ

Acute ROM gain per session. Chronic trend over weeks. The metric that matters most.

Product Roadmap

Honest scope. No overclaiming.

V1 — Launch

Four stretch patterns

  • Hamstring SLR (straight-leg raise)
  • Adductor abduction
  • Hip flexor extension
  • PNF contract-relax overlay

Same motor, same cable, same pulley for all four. The athlete changes position — the machine stays simple.

V2 — Year 2

Three additional patterns

  • Hip internal / external rotation
  • Ankle dorsiflexion
  • Thoracic rotation

Requires adjustable pulley position on frame (sliding track). Same motor and cable system. Software update + hardware bracket.

Intellectual Property

Three patentable claims.

Claim 1: Force-controlled stretch with compensation-aware adjustment

A method for automated assisted stretching comprising: applying controlled cable tension via PID force loop while simultaneously detecting joint compensation through depth-camera pose estimation, and modifying tension in response to detected compensation.

Claim 2: Integrated cable-drive + vision feedback apparatus

An apparatus combining: motorized cable spool with inline force sensing, depth camera with skeletal tracking, and a compliance controller that reduces stretch load when non-target joint movement exceeds a threshold.

Claim 3: Bilateral assessment + adaptive protocol system

A system for bilateral ROM assessment and progressive protocol adaptation comprising: standardized force application, bilateral comparison logic, readiness-adjusted intensity selection, and longitudinal trend analysis with automated asymmetry alerting.
