The Problem

Athletes lose games — and careers — to injuries that were preventable.

Strength has force plates. Speed has timing gates. Recovery has Normatec. But flexibility — the single biggest lever for injury prevention and on-field performance — is still estimated by eye and documented on paper.

  • No measurement → manual goniometry has 5–10° error; tight hamstrings and hip asymmetries go undetected until an athlete pulls up (Norkin & White, Clinical Measurement of Joint Motion, 2016)
  • No consistency → no standardized protocol, no force control, no way to know if today's stretch matched yesterday's — or the athlete next to them (NSCA, Essentials of Strength Training, 2024)
  • No longitudinal data → when an athlete tears a hamstring, there's no flexibility trend data to analyze — the warning signs were never captured (British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2023)

The cost is already measured — just not prevented:

• Muscle injuries account for ~31% of all time-loss injuries in elite football — the largest single injury category. (Ekstrand, Hägglund & Waldén, Br J Sports Med 2011)

• Hamstring strains are the #1 most common soft-tissue injury in the NFL, with incidence rising year-over-year despite strength and conditioning advances. (Feeley et al., Am J Sports Med 2008)

• Financial cost per hamstring injury in elite football averages ~$405K per incident in lost player value and wages. (Hickey, Shield, Williams & Opar, Br J Sports Med 2014)

How It Works

Eight minutes. Nine variables. Zero guesswork.

1. Badge in

Athlete scans ID. System loads their profile, history, and today's training context.

2. Baseline screen

Standardized passive stretch at 8 lb each side. Bilateral ROM comparison in 60 seconds.

3. Stretch protocol

Motor ramps cable tension. PID loop maintains constant force. Camera tracks compensation. Data logs every second.

4. Session report

ROM gain, asymmetry change, tolerance trend. Exportable. Comparable. Actionable.

5

Technology

Five systems. One closed loop.

Cable Drive

200W BLDC motor, 20:1 planetary gearbox, Dyneema cable. ODrive FOC controller at 100Hz. Force-compliant, backdriveable.

Depth Camera

Intel RealSense D435. 12-point skeletal tracking at 30fps. Measures joint angles to ±2°. Detects compensation in real time.

Live Interface

15" touchscreen. Real-time force graph, ROM tracking, bilateral comparison. Dark UI designed for staff and athlete visibility.

Quick-Release Cuff

Padded neoprene. Carabiner quick-release. 5-layer safety: software cap, PID compliance, slip clutch, E-stop, quick-release.

6

What It Measures

Nine variables. Measured, not estimated.

Joint Angle

Peak passive ROM at standardized force. ±2° accuracy. Comparable across sessions.

Cable Force

Inline load cell at 100Hz. Logs tension ramp, peak force, and variability during hold.

Asymmetry

Left vs. right at matched force. Flags differences >10%. Tracks resolution over time.

Hold Time

Duration at end-range without retreating. A neuromuscular tolerance metric.

Compensation

Camera detects pelvic rotation, knee bend, trunk shift. Quantified, not eyeballed.

Tolerance Threshold

The force level where the athlete first reports discomfort or resists. Tracked over weeks.

Pain Response

Binary flag per rep. Correlated with force and ROM to build a tissue-response profile.

Tension Ramp

Rate of force acceptance during initial stretch. Indicates tissue readiness and stiffness.

Session Δ

Acute ROM gain per session. Chronic trend over weeks. The metric that matters most.

Product Roadmap

  • Hip flexor extension
  • PNF contract-relax overlay

  • Ankle dorsiflexion
