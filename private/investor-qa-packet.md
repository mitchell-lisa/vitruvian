## 7. Exit scenarios

**Three credible paths, in rough order of likelihood:**

### A. Strategic acquisition — the base case
**Acquirers:** Hyperice, Catapult, Nike, TeamWorx (Smartabase parent), VALD parent (MQ Scientific).

**Why:** the patent + data corpus + AMS integrations are the asset. These acquirers already own distribution into pro + collegiate performance staffs. They do NOT own a measurement-plus-intervention surface. Modulor fills the hole.

**Trigger:** $10M+ ARR, 50K+ athlete-months in corpus, published clinical validation.

**Range:** 6–12x ARR = $60M–$120M. Higher if the data-licensing narrative lands with insurance partners before acquisition.

### B. Vertical insurance licensing — the upside case
**Partner:** WC / disability carriers (Travelers, Liberty Mutual, AIG). $70B+ US market. MSK = ~40% of claims.

**Why:** once the hazard-ratio validation is peer-reviewed, carriers buy aggregated risk signals — not hardware. The data corpus is the product; Modulor becomes an actuarial-infrastructure company.

**Trigger:** peer-reviewed clinical paper + 10K+ athlete-months in corpus. Earliest Year 3.

**Range:** 10–20x revenue. Company revalues as a data-infrastructure business, not a hardware business.

### C. IPO path — the tail case
**Trigger:** $100M+ ARR across Teams + Insurance + DoD. Dual-track data infrastructure + hardware story.

**Why it's a tail case:** longer horizon, requires both pillars to materialize. Not what we're underwriting the seed on. But the market size supports it.

**Range:** public comparable = Catapult Group (ASX:CAT) at ~6x revenue. Modulor with insurance licensing + DoD revenue would trade richer.
