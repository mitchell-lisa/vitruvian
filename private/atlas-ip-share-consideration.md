## Why this record exists

At formation, Modulor, Inc. founders pay for their shares using intellectual
property they developed prior to incorporation, per Stripe Atlas's default
Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement (RSPA) template. This file documents that
this was Atlas's stated default and the founder's deliberate choice to accept
it, given the de minimis fair market value of the pre-formation IP.

## FMV justification (pre-revenue, pre-validation)

At the time of incorporation, the IP listed above has a fair market value of
approximately **$0–$100** because:

1. The patent is **provisional only** — not issued, not defensible, 12-month
   placeholder
2. **No commercialization** — no product built, no prototype shipped
3. **No revenue, no customers, no licensing deals**
4. **No prior valuation event** — no investor, no acquisition offer, no
   licensing inquiry
5. **No issued patents and no granted IP rights**

Per IRS guidance, formation-stage IP transfers are not typically challenged
absent a valuation trigger (prior sale, revenue, issued/granted IP with
established licensing). This grant falls comfortably within standard
pre-seed founder IP-assignment practice.

## Atlas exchange — verbatim

**Question asked via Ask Atlas (April 20, 2026):**

> I've filed a provisional patent (VITR-001) and developed product
> specifications before forming. I want to pay cash for my founder shares
> and execute a separate IP assignment agreement for the pre-existing IP,
> rather than using IP as share consideration. Can Atlas accommodate?

**Atlas response (verbatim):**

> Atlas automatically enables founders to pay for shares using the
> intellectual property (IP) they have developed. However, if you want to
> pay cash for your founder shares and execute a separate IP assignment
> agreement, you may need to work with a legal professional to create that
> specific arrangement. Atlas generally expects IP related to the company
> to be contributed at the time of incorporation.

## Decision

Founder elects to **proceed with Atlas's default** IP-as-share-consideration
path. Rationale:

- Pre-formation IP value is de minimis for the reasons documented above
- Cost / time of engaging external counsel for a custom cash + separate IP
  assignment arrangement is not justified at this stage
- Atlas's RSPA template is industry-standard and was drafted in
  collaboration with Cooley LLP
